| [[p. 270]] 3, 14. Haec tibi scribo sperans cito me venire ad te; 15. si autem tardavero, ut scias quemadmodum oporteat te in domo dei conversari, quae est ecclesia dei vivi, columna et firmamentum veritatis. 1. quantum ad scientiam eius pertinebat, hoc erat in eo quod citius illum visurus esset, sed quia solet aliud deo placere, cuius providentia ipsi soli cognita est, idcirco ne forte, inquit, tardem, scribo tibi, ut scias quomodo ecclesiam ordines, quae est domus dei, ut cum totus mundus dei sit, ecclesia tamen domus eius dicatur, cuius hodie rector est Damasus. 2. mundus enim in praevaricatione est diverso turbatus errore. ideo illic necesse est dicatur esse domus dei et veritas, ubi secundum voluntatem suam timetur; hoc profitentibus de eo servis, quod ipse dignatus est edocere, ne forte verba eius esse negarentur, virtutibus haec signavit, quae a nullo alio fieri possunt, ut his infidelitas accusata inexcusabilis pereat. firmamentum enim veritatis huius signa sunt et prodigia. | | 3:14. These things I write to you, hoping to come to you soon; 15. but if I should be delayed, [I write] that you may know how one ought to conduct oneself in the house of God, which is the church of the living God, the pillar and ground of the truth. 1. As far as his own knowledge went, his expectation was this — that he would see him rather soon; but because it is wont that something else should please God, whose providence is known to Himself alone, therefore — lest perchance, he says, I be delayed — I write to you, that you may know how you are to order the church, which is the house of God: so that, although the whole world is God's, the church is nevertheless called His house — whose ruler today is Damasus. 2. For the world is in transgression, thrown into confusion by manifold error. Therefore the house of God, and the truth, must be said to be there where He is feared according to His own will; and, since the servants profess concerning Him that which He Himself deigned to teach, He sealed these things — lest perchance His words should be denied to be His — with mighty works that can be done by no one else, so that by them unbelief, being convicted, may perish without excuse. For the signs and portents are the ground of this truth.  |

| [[p. 98]] 4, 11. Et ipse dedit quosdam quidem apostolos, quosdam autem profetas, alios vero evangelistas, quosdam vero pastores et magistros 12. ad consummationem sanctorum in opus ministerii. 1. apostoli episcopi sunt; profetae vero explanatores sunt scripturarum. quamvis inter ipsa primordia fuerint profetae sicut Agabus et quattuor virgines profetantes, sicut continetur in Actis apostolorum, {sed} propter rudimenta fidei conmendanda; nunc autem interpretes profetae dicuntur. evangelistae diacones (diaconi) sunt, sicut fuit Filippus. quamquam non sint sacerdotes, evangelizare tamen possunt sine cathedra quemadmodum et Stefanus et Filippus memoratus [[p. 99]] pastores possunt esse lectores, qui lectionibus saginent populum audientem, quia non in pane tantum vivet homo, sed in omni verbo dei. 2. magistri vero exorcistae sunt, quia in ecclesia ipsi conpescunt et verberant inquietos, sive hi qui lectionibus inbuendi infantes solebant inbuere, sicut mos Iudaeorum est, quorum traditio ad nos transitum fecit, quae per neglegentiam obsolevit. inter istos post episcopum plus esse intellegitur, qui propter reseratum occultum scripturarum sensum profetare dicitur, praesertim quia futurae spei verba depromit, qui ordo non potest esse presbyterii. nam in episcopo omnes ordines sunt, qui et (quia) primus sacerdos est, hoc est princeps sacerdotum, et profeta et evangelista et cetera ad implenda officia ecclesiae in ministerio fidelium. 3. tamen postquam omnibus locis ecclesiae sunt constitutae et officia ordinata, aliter conposita res est quam coeperat. primum enim omnes docebant et omnes baptizabant, quibuscumque diebus vel temporibus fuisset occasio. nec enim Filippus tempus quaesivit aut diem, quo eunuchum baptizaret, neque ieiunium interposuit neque Paulus et Sileas tempus distulerunt, quo optionem carceris baptizarent cum omnibus eius neque Petrus clericos habuit aut diem quaesivit, quando Cornelium cum omni domu sua (eius) baptizavit nec ipse, sed iussit fratribus, qui cum illo ierant ad Cornelium ab Ioppe; adhuc enim praeter septem diacones (diaconos) nullus fuerat ordinatus. 4. ut ergo cresceret plebs et multiplicaretur, omnibus inter initia [[p. 100]] concessum est et evangelizare et baptizare et scripturas in ecclesia explanare. at ubi autem omnia loca circumplexa est ecclesia, conventicula constituta sunt et rectores et cetera officia ecclesiis sunt ordinata, ut nullus de clero auderet, qui ordinatus non esset, praesumere officium, quod sciret non sibi creditum vel concessum. et coepit alio ordine et providentia ecclesia gubernari (gubernari ecclesia), quia, si omnes eadem possent, inrationabile esset et vulgaris res et vilissima videretur. hinc ergo est, unde nunc neque diaconi in populo praedicant neque ceteri vel laici baptizant neque quocumque die credentes tinguntur nisi aegri. 5. ideo non per omnia conveniunt scripta apostoli ordinationi, quae nunc in ecclesia est, quia haec inter ipsa primordia sunt scripta. nam et Timotheum presbyterum a se creatum episcopum vocat, quia primi presbyteri episcopi appellabantur, ut recedente eo sequens ei succederet. denique apud Aegyptum presbyteri consignant, si praesens non sit episcopus. sed quia coeperunt sequentes presbyteri indigni inveniri ad primatos tenendos, inmutata est ratio prospiciente consilio, ut non ordo, sed meritum crearet episcopum multorum sacerdotum iudicio constitutum, ne indignus temere usurparet et esset multis scandalum. 6. in lege nascebantur sacerdotes ex genere Aaron levitae. nunc enim omnes sunt ex genere (ex genere sunt) sacerdotali dicente Petro apostolo: quia sumus (estis), inquit, genus regale et sacerdotale; ideoque ex populo potest fieri sacerdos. | | 4:11. And He Himself gave some indeed to be apostles, some however prophets, others truly evangelists, some truly pastors and teachers, 12. for the perfecting of the saints unto the work of the ministry. 1. The apostles are bishops; the prophets, however, are expounders of the scriptures. Although in the very beginnings there were prophets — such as Agabus and the four prophesying virgins, as is contained in the Acts of the Apostles, {but} for the sake of commending the rudiments of the faith — now, however, they are called interpreters [rather than "prophets"]. The evangelists are deacons, as was Philip. Although they are not priests, they can nevertheless evangelize without a [bishop's] chair, just as Stephen too and the aforementioned Philip. The pastors can be lectors, who feed with readings the listening people, because man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word of God. 2. The teachers, however, are exorcists, because in the church it is they who restrain and chastise the unquiet — or [they are] those who used to imbue with readings the infants [i.e. catechumens] that were to be imbued, as is the custom of the Jews, whose tradition passed over to us, [though] it has fallen out of use through neglect. Among these, [the one] next after the bishop is understood to be greater, who, on account of the unlocked hidden sense of the scriptures, is said to prophesy — especially because he brings forth words of future hope — which order cannot be that of the presbyterate. For in the bishop are all the orders, since he is also (because he is) the first priest, that is, the chief of priests, and prophet and evangelist and the rest, for the fulfilling of the offices of the church in the ministry of the faithful. 3. Nevertheless, after the churches were established in all places and the offices ordained, the matter was arranged otherwise than it had begun. For at first all taught and all baptized, on whatever days or times there was occasion. For Philip did not seek a time or a day on which to baptize the eunuch, nor interpose a fast; nor did Paul and Silas defer the time in which to baptize the jailer with all his [household]; nor did Peter have clerics or seek a day when he baptized Cornelius with his whole house — nor did he [baptize] himself, but bade the brethren who had gone with him to Cornelius from Joppa; for as yet, besides the seven deacons, no one had been ordained. 4. So then, that the people might grow and be multiplied, it was granted to all, in the beginnings, both to evangelize and to baptize and to expound the scriptures in the church. But when the church had embraced all places, assemblies were established, and rectors and the other offices were ordained for the churches, so that no one of the clergy who had not been ordained should dare to presume an office which he knew had not been entrusted or granted to him. And the church began to be governed by another order and providence, because, if all could do the same things, it would be unreasonable, and the thing would seem common and most cheap. Hence, then, it is, that now neither do deacons preach among the people, nor do the rest, or laymen, baptize, nor on any [chance] day are believers washed except the sick. 5. Therefore the writings of the Apostle do not in all respects agree with the ordinance which is now in the church, because these things were written in the very beginnings. For he even calls Timothy, a presbyter ordained by himself, a bishop, because the first presbyters were called bishops, so that, when the one departed, the next should succeed him. Finally, in Egypt the presbyters seal [i.e. confirm], if a bishop be not present. But because the following presbyters began to be found unworthy to hold the primacies, the plan was changed, foresight providing for it, so that not order [seniority] but merit should make a bishop, appointed by the judgment of many priests, lest an unworthy man should rashly usurp [the office] and be a scandal to many. 6. Under the law, priests were born of the stock of Aaron, [being] Levites. But now all are of a priestly stock, as Peter the apostle says: "For we are," he says, "a royal and priestly race"; and therefore from [among] the people a priest can be made. |

Rogamus autem vos, fratres, super adventu domini nostri Iesu Christi et nostrae congregationis in ipsum, 2. ne facile moveamini a sensu vestro neque conturbemini, neque per spiritum neque per verbum neque per epistolam tamquam a nobis missam, quasi instet dies domini. 3. ne quis vos seducat ullo modo, quia nisi venerit defectio primum et revelatus fuerit homo ille peccati, filius perditionis, 4. qui adversatur [[p. 239]] et extollitur super omnem qui dicitur deus, aut quod colitur, ita ut in templo dei sedeat, ostendens se ipsum quasi sit deus. 1. obsecrat eos ne leviter et facile de adventu quasi inminentis domini opinionem reciperent, et talia dat mandata, ut etiam si per spiritum aliquis velut profeta fuerit locutus, credi ei non debeat, neque si per tractatum aut per epistolam nomine forte apostolorum scriptam, adsensum tribuendum. solent enim tergiversatores, ut fallant, sub nomine clari alicuius viri epistolam fingere, ut auctoritas nominis possit conmendare quod per se ipsum recipi non posset. hoc ideo, ne ipsa perturbatione, dum incauti invenirentur, possent seduci ad adorandum diabolum, qui idcirco haec iactabit, ut sub nomine salvatoris (salvatoris nomine) apparens ac fallens sanctos velit se adorari, ut decipiat credentes in Christum. 2. sed ut locum aut occasionem fallendi, quam se putat habere, non habeat inpudentissimus satanas, tempus et signa adventus domini designavit, quia non prius veniet dominus, quam regni Romani defectio fiat et [ante] appareat Antichristus, qui interficiet sanctos, reddita Romanis libertate, sub suo tamen nomine. sciens enim venturum dominum ad se conprimendum nomen eius sibi usurpabit, et ut regnum eius verum esse videatur et trahat secum qui simul cum eo pereant, ita ut in domo domini in sede sedeat Christi, ut ipsum deum se adserat, non filium dei. 3. unde in evangelio ad Iudaeos dominus ait: ego veni in [[p. 240]] nomine patris mei et non me recepistis; [He says] this for this reason: lest by that very disturbance, while [men] were found off their guard, they might be seduced into worshipping the devil — who for this reason will vaunt these things, that, appearing under the name of the Savior and deceiving the saints, he may wish to be worshipped, so as to deceive those who believe in Christ. 2. | [[p. 271]] 5. Dehinc adiecit: et in catedra pestilentiae non sedit. hanc dicit beatitudinem esse, quae his tribus gradibus constat et triplici ratione munitur, id est, ut neque in consilium impiorum eatur neque in uia peccatorum stetur neque in catedra pestilentiae sedeatur. sed cum duo genera tantum habeantur, impiorum et [[p. 272]] peccatorum, in reprehensione, quae supra memorata sunt, hoc tertium cui adscribi uoluit, quod adiecit dicens: et in catedra pestilentiae non sedit? impiorum aut peccatorum? sed uideamus, quid sit catedram pestilentiae sedere, et tunc cui generi hoc adscribat intellegemus. hanc dicimus catedram pestilentiae, quae extra dei ordinationem est, quae ad hoc utique inuenta est, quae, ut iniqua inde exeant iudicia, propterea pestilentiae catedra dicta est, quae est corruptio quae parit mortem, sicut et iniquitas damnationem. non est ergo a deo quae est catedra mortis; nam Moyses accepit catedram uitae. ad hoc enim data est, ut auctoritas in ea sit iusti iudicis uel creatoris dei. unde dicit dominus: super catedram Moysi sederunt scribae et Farisaei; et apostolus: non est, inquit, potestas nisi a deo; quae enim sunt, a deo ordinatae sunt. | | 5. Next he added: "and has not sat in the seat of pestilence." He says that this is the blessedness which stands upon these three degrees and is fortified by a threefold rationale — namely, that one neither go into the counsel of the ungodly, nor stand in the way of sinners, nor sit in the seat of pestilence. But since only two kinds [of men] are held [in view] — of the ungodly and of sinners — in the censure of the things mentioned above, to which [kind] did he wish this third to be assigned, which he added, saying: "and has not sat in the seat of pestilence"? To the ungodly, or to sinners? But let us see what it is to sit in the seat of pestilence, and then we shall understand to which kind he assigns this. We call that the seat of pestilence which is outside the ordinance of God, which has been devised precisely for this — that unjust judgments may issue from it; for that reason it is called the seat of pestilence, which is a corruption that begets death, just as iniquity [begets] damnation. That, therefore, is not from God which is the seat of death; for Moses received the seat of life. For it was given for this: that in it there should be the authority of a just judge, or [rather] of God the creator. Whence the Lord says: "Upon the seat of Moses have sat the scribes and Pharisees"; and the Apostle: "There is no power," he says, "but from God; for those that are, are ordained by God." |

Unde dicit ad principem plebis: tu quidem sedes iudicans secundum legem et contra legem iubes me percuti? quod dixit 'secundum legem', iustam et salutarem catedrae auctoritatem significauit. illud autem quod ait: contra legem iubes me percuti, illum ipsum iniustum iudicem ostendit, ut in dei catedra sedens iudicaret iniuste. hinc est unde et Danihel: dei est, ait, regnum et cui uult dabit illud. sicut ergo terreni imperatoris auctoritas currit per omnes ut in omnibus eius sit reuerentia, ita deus instituit, ut ab ipso rege dei auctoritas incipiat et currat per cunctos. quamuis frequenter mundus hoc non intellegat et alii se subiciat in potestate positus quam debet, tamen institutio est ut unus sit, [[p. 273]] qui timeatur. ubi ergo haec institutio non est, ibi catedra pestilentiae repperitur. nusquam unius dei auctoritas abnuitur, nisi apud eos. qui multorum deorum praedicant metum et reuerentiam. hi sunt qui in catedra pestilentiae sedent, quia quod pontifices eorum praedicant mors est. hoc enim docent, ut cultores unius dei quasi stulti et inimici aut obprobrio sint aut de hac uita tollantur. haec ipsa materia impietatis est, quae etiam in ceteras partes extenditur, per quas impietatis suae leges ostendat. nam iussu Iezabel antestites iniquitatis in catedra pestilentiae sederunt, qui aduersus innocentem Nabuthae, cum iudices non essent, praua meditantes subornatis falsis testibus mortis tulerunt sententiam. unde in alio psalmo dicit: et cum impiis non sedebo. hoc enim iudicium eius est, cuius et catedra pestilentiae. itaque si in dei catedra sedentes innocentes opprimant, iniustum erit iudicium, non catedra. ubi enim catedra pestilentiae est, non potest iudicium non esse iniquum, quamuis quaedam adplicent sibi uelut uelamina, quibus impietatem contegant, ut uidentes fallantur. non potest enim sola impietas proponi, quia non inuenit emptorem, et, ubi lex diaboli est, si ipse nominetur, horretur. | | 6. Whence he says to the ruler of the people: "Do you indeed sit judging according to the law, and against the law order me to be struck?" In that he said "according to the law," he signified the just and salutary authority of the seat. But that which he says — "against the law you order me to be struck" — shows that judge himself to be unjust, in that, sitting in the seat of God, he judged unjustly. Hence it is that Daniel too [says]: "The kingdom is God's," he says, "and to whom He wills He will give it." As, therefore, the authority of an earthly emperor runs through all, so that in all there may be reverence for him, so God has ordained that from the king himself the authority of God should begin and run through all. Although frequently the world does not understand this, and one set in power subjects himself [to another] more than he ought, nevertheless the ordinance is that there be one who is feared. Where, therefore, this ordinance is not, there the seat of pestilence is found. Nowhere is the authority of the one God refused, except among those who preach the fear and reverence of many gods. These are they who sit in the seat of pestilence, because what their pontiffs preach is death. For they teach this: that the worshippers of the one God should either — as fools and enemies — be held in reproach, or be taken out of this life. This very thing is the matter of impiety, which extends also into other areas, through which it may set forth the laws of its own impiety. For at Jezebel's command the presiders of iniquity sat in the seat of pestilence, who, against innocent Naboth, though they were not judges, plotting wicked things, by suborned false witnesses passed a sentence of death. Whence in another psalm he says: "and I will not sit with the ungodly." For this is his judgment, whose also is the seat of pestilence. And so, if, sitting in the seat of God, they oppress the innocent, it will be the judgment that is unjust, not the seat. For where the seat of pestilence is, the judgment cannot but be iniquitous — although they apply to themselves certain veils, as it were, with which to cover their impiety, so that the seeing may be deceived. For impiety cannot be set forth alone, because it finds no buyer; and, where the law of the devil is, if it be named [openly], it is shuddered at. |

Sicut ueneno admiscetur per quod lateat, ita et legi diaboli quaedam iusta admiscent et nomen adplicant ueritatis, sed unius cuiusque sensus est iudicandus, quia ideo aliquando aliquid uerum ostendit, ut fallat, ut, cum maligna praedicat, bona putentur. quoniam catedram pestilentiae non esse de [[p. 274]] dei ordinatione adseuerauimus, etiam eorum qui extra ecclesiam uel contra ecclesiam sedes sibi instituerunt, catedram pestilentiae esse dicimus. qui enim inconcessa praesumit, reus est: quanto magis si et corrumpat traditionem eius, cuius sedem usurpat! nam et ordinem ab apostolo Petro coeptum et usque ad hoc tempus per traducem succedentium episcoporum seruatum perturbant ordinem sibi sine origine uindicantes, hoc est corpus sine capite profitentes; unde congruit etiam eorum sedem catedram pestilentiae appellare. nec enim ideo impune erit, quia sub dei nomine haec agunt. constat enim suas illos causas sub dei nomine agere. non enim zelo dei haec agunt, sed sibi locum uolentes defendere, cum sciamus Chore et CCL uiros per praesumptionem deo offerentes hiatu terrae absortos esse et Oziam regem, cum deo inlicite obtulisset, lepra in fronte percussum. | | 7. Just as [poison] is mixed in with something through which it may lie hidden, so also to the law of the devil they mix in certain just things and apply the name of truth; but the sense of each single [statement] must be judged, because it sometimes shows something true precisely in order to deceive — so that, when it proclaims malignant things, they may be thought good. Since we have asserted that the seat of pestilence is not from the ordinance of God, we say that the seat of those also who, outside the church or against the church, have set up seats for themselves, is a seat of pestilence. For he who presumes upon things not permitted is guilty: how much more if he also corrupts the tradition of him whose seat he usurps! For they disturb the order begun from the apostle Peter and preserved down to this present time through the propagative succession of bishops following one upon another, claiming for themselves an order without origin — that is, professing a body without a head; whence it is fitting to call their seat too a seat of pestilence. Nor will it therefore go unpunished, that they do these things under the name of God. For it is agreed that those men plead their own causes under the name of God. For they do not do these things out of zeal for God, but wishing to defend a place for themselves — since we know that Korah and the two hundred and fifty men, offering to God through presumption, were swallowed up by the gaping of the earth, and that King Uzziah, when he had offered unlawfully to God, was struck with leprosy on the forehead.  |
